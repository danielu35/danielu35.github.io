Rule Of Thirds

Photo on Canons page

usa.canon.com

I chose the Rule Of Thirds because i thought it was simple principle yet very helpful! The reason that I say it is helpful is because I often struggle with knowing what pictures to use on anything. The rule of thirds gives a good and simple understanding of what people may be looking for or what can catch peoples eyes. The rule of thirds are two horizantle lines and two vertical lines that make a grid of 9 blocks. The likes of where the two blocks meet are called strong points and are locations that can catch the eye of users.

Hick's Law

The Champions League

UEFA.com

Hicks Law is a pretty cool princlple that every one to an extent uses. Can you imagine going to a web page and there is no menu bars of sub tabs to make it easier for the user? Imagine that all the options on a page where just listed in no particualar order, not even alphabetised. Hick's law is sometimes cited to justify menu design decisions. I chose the UEFA website because the page its self has a lot of info but for most users the only thing they care about is the score of the games and the schedule for the tournament. Hicks law makes it easy for the user to find that info they need with out looking through multiple links.
